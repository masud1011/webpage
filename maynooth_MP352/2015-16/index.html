Material, sources

MP352 lecture notes of Prof. Charles Nash --- this is roughly the material to be covered in the module, but there will be some differences. The ordering of topics in this semesters' lectures is likely to be very different.

I strongly suggest that you work through these notes and at least one other textbook. Special Relativity challenges our intuition and takes some time to digest; so do try to read a fair bit every week.

I listed some textbooks in the first class. Feel free to pick any. In particular, I recommend

Chapters 12 -- 14 of
Kleppner & Kolenkow, Introduction to Mechanics, 2nd ed. Cambridge Univ Press

I will loosely follow the order of the Kleppner-Kolenkow chapters. The derivation of the Lorentz transformation discussed in class follows this text. There are several copies of the book in the university library.

There are many, many other textbooks covering Special Relativity at a level not too far from this module. Examples:

Chapters 15 -- 17 of
The Feynman Lectures on Physics, Volume I

Chapter 10 of
Griffiths, Introduction to Electrodynamics

Chapters 1 -- 5 of
Rindler, Relativity: Special, General, and Cosmological

Chapters 1 -- 4 of
Woodhouse, Special Relativity

Chapters 29 -- 34 of
Greiner, Classical Mechanics: Point Particles and Relativity

Chapters 11 -- 13 of
Morin, Introduction to Classical Mechanics
