Material covered in Class

I point to relevant chapters in Prof. Nash's Notes and in Vol. II of the Feynman lectures (referred to as Feynman II below).
Of course, equivalent material is available in many other textbooks, or in online material such as those linked to further down on this page.

Electromagnetic field energy.
Chapter 27 in Feynman II.   Nash notes: chapter VI.

Maxwell's equations, displacement current density, wave solutions.
Chapters 18 and 20 in Feynman II.   Nash notes: chapters V and VI.

Electromagnetic Induction; Faraday's law.
Chapters 16 and 17 in Feynman II.   Nash notes: chapter V.

Vector Potential.
Chapter 15 in Feynman II.   Unfortunately, Nash notes do not discuss the vector potential.

Magnetic field.
Chapters 13 and 14 in Feynman II.   Nash notes: chapter IV.

Electric Currents.
Chapter 13 in Feynman II.   Nash notes: chapter III.

Applications of Gauss' law.
In Feynman II, Chapter 5 is highly recommended reading.   In Nash notes, this is chapter II.

Electric flux. Gauss' dielectric flux theorem (a.k.a. Gauss' law).
In Feynman II, the discussion of flux begins in Chapter 4 Section 5, and continues through Chapter 5.   Nash-notes covers this material in Chapter 2.

Coulomb's Law, Electric Fields, Electric Potentials.
Nash notes: chapter 1.
In Feynman II, you will find similar material in the first 4 sections of Chapter 4.
The introduction to the electric potential in Nash-notes Chapter 1 Section 3 is more detailed than we had time for in class; you might want to read this carefully.

Overview and Background.
In Feynman II, Chapter 1 gives an overview of what we will learn this semester.
Chapters 2 and 3 introduces grad-div-curl and vector integration. This is essential background for MP204; it is assumed that you have previously mastered most of this material. Working through these chapters will probably help you greatly in MP204.

Textbooks

There are many, many textbooks on introductory electromagnetism or electrodynamics. You are strongly encouraged to work through one or more textbooks.

In particular, I will point to chapters/sections in the Feynman lectures (Volume II), which are free to read on
this website.
The material we will cover in MP204 is mostly contained within the first 20 chapters of Volume II. (Specifically: Chapters 1, 4--6, 13--18, 20.)

I point to Feynman Vol. II because it is freely available to read. However, the material is very standard and has not changed much in the past 50 years. You will find the same topics covered in many other texts.

Some other texts are listed below. Our library carries a number of these in paper copy and a few more as e-books.

  • Chabay & Sherwood, Matter & Interactions, Vol. II.

  • Griffiths, Introduction to Electrodynamics.

  • Purcell and Morin, Electricity and Magnetism.

  • Panofsky & Phillips, Classical Electricity and Magnetism.

  • Grant & Phillips, Electromagnetism.

  • Fleisch, A Student's Guide to Maxwell's Equations.

  • Edminister & Nahvi, Schaum's Outline of Electromagnetics.

  • Shankar, Fundamentals of Physics II: Electromagnetism, Optics, and Quantum Mechanics.

Notation

We will use SI (also called MKS or MKSA) units. Note that many equations look quite different when written in Gaussian (or CGS) units. When reading a textbook, be sure to watch out for which units that text is using.

Notations vary. I will mostly try using the same notations as in Prof. Nash's notes, but will not always succeed. You anyway need to be able to read and learn from multiple sources using different notations for the same physical quantities.
